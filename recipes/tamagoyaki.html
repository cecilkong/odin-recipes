tamagoyaki

Description

Tamagoyaki is a type of Japanese omelette made by rolling together several layers of fried egg.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 Tbsp neutral-flavored oil
  • 1 ½ sheet nori (seaweed)/li>
  • 3 Tbsp dashi
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp mirin
  • 2 pinch kosher salt
  • 3 oz daikon radish

Steps

  1. Gently whisk the eggs in a bowl. It's best to "cut" the eggs with chopsticks in a zig-zag motion and do not over mix.
  2. In another bowl, combine the seasonings and mix well.
  3. Pour the seasonings mixture into the egg mixture and whisk gently. Then pour the mixture into a measuring cup with spout and handle (so that it'll be easier to pour into the frying pan).
  4. Heat the pan over medium heat, dip a folded paper towel in oil and apply to the pan. Put a little bit of egg mixture to see if the pan is hot.
  5. When you hear the sizzling sound, pour a thin layer of egg mixture in the pan, tilting to cover the bottom of the pan.
  6. Poke the air bubbles to release the air. After the bottom of the egg has set but still soft on top, start rolling into a log shape from one side to the other.
  7. Move the rolled omelette to the side where you started to roll, and apply oil to the pan with a paper towel, even under the omelette.
  8. Pour the egg mixture to cover the bottom of the pan again. Make sure to lift the omelette to spread the mixture underneath.
  9. When the new layer of egg has set and still soft on top, start rolling from one side to the other.
  10. Repeat steps 8 and 9 until all of the whisked eggs are used up.
  11. Remove from the pan and place the omelette on the bamboo sushi mat and wrap it up. Shape the egg when it is still hot. Let it stand for 5 minutes.
  12. To serve, slice the omelette into ½" (1 cm) pieces and grate some daikon over the omelette.
